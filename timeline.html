Archive History

A timeline of resistance.

1969

Founding of La Casa Cultural

Established as a space for Latinx students to build community and advocate for their presence at Yale.

1974

MEChA de Yale

Formal organization of student activism and cultural preservation initiatives begins.

1980s

Expansion of Programming

Cultural events, speaker series, and community outreach grow significantly under new student leadership.

1990s

Institutional Growth

Expanded programming and the formalization of the archive collection starts as alumni return to share stories.

2000s

Digital Beginnings

Early efforts to digitize flyers, photographs, and organizational records from prior decades.

2010s

HP&E Committee Formed

The Historical Preservation & Education committee is established to formally steward the community's memory.

2019

50 Years of Resistance

The golden anniversary marks a half-century of community building and the launch of the digital preservation project.

Present

This Archive

Community members, alumni, and students actively contribute to build a living record of La Casa's history.
