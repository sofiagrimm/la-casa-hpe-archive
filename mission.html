About the Archive

Why we preserve.

The HP&E Archive exists to ensure that the history of the Latine community at Yale is never again at risk of erasure.

La Casa Cultural de Julia de Burgos was founded in 1969 through the direct advocacy of Puerto Rican and Chicano students who demanded institutional recognition and space. What followed was five decades of organizing, cultural production, and community building that shaped the university in profound ways.

The Historical Preservation & Education (HP&E) committee was established to ensure that this legacy is documented, accessible, and taught to future generations. Too much has already been lost to the transience of student life and the indifference of institutional memory.

This archive is a corrective act.

Our commitments

Preservation

Digitizing and cataloguing physical materials — flyers, photographs, meeting minutes — before they are lost to time.

Access

Making the archive freely available to current students, alumni, researchers, and the broader community.

Education

Developing programming, guides, and curricula that connect the archive to living questions of identity and justice.

Community

Centering the voices of those who lived this history, not just the institutions that recorded it.
